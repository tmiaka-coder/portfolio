AI Janken App

A real-time "Rock-Paper-Scissors" game against AI using browser-based hand tracking. Experience advanced image analysis and serverless execution with MediaPipe Hands.

01

Project Overview

AI Janken is a browser-based application that recognizes human hand gestures in real-time to play against an AI opponent. By leveraging Google's MediaPipe Hands on the frontend, the system achieves low-latency image analysis without a dedicated backend server. As a PWA (Progressive Web App), it provides a native-like experience without the need for installation.

02

Background & Purpose

This project began as a challenge: "How can AI technology be integrated into everyday play to maximize UX?" I aimed to create a tool where anyone can experience the power of AI instantly via a single URL, removing the psychological hurdles of complex equipment or app store downloads.

03

Key Focus: UI and AI Logic

Mobile Optimization & Immersive Layout

Standard video layouts often separate the player and CPU vertically on mobile screens, breaking the sense of "confrontation." I utilized CSS Grid to maintain a horizontal side-by-side layout even on mobile, minimizing eye movement and creating an intuitive, face-to-face gameplay feel.

High-Precision Hand Tracking

I implemented an algorithm that analyzes 21 landmark coordinates from MediaPipe Hands. By calculating the relative positions of finger tips and joints in JavaScript, I developed a custom logic to determine gestures based on the "count of standing fingers."

05

Design Philosophy & Commitment

Accessibility and UX Consistency

Following my "Accessibility-First" principle, I utilized aria-live attributes so that screen readers immediately announce AI judgments and game results. PWA implementation further enhances user convenience by allowing offline access and direct launching from the home screen.

Privacy & Security

The design is "Privacy-Focused," completing all image analysis on the client-side within the browser. No video data is ever sent to a server. For security, I strictly used textContent for DOM updates to prevent injection attacks.
