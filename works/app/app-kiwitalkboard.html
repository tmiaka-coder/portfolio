App Development

Kiwi Talkboard

A secure, thread-based community board connecting Japanese residents in New Zealand. Built with PHP & MySQL backend and Tailwind CSS for a modern, near-realtime posting experience.

01

Project Overview

Kiwi Talkboard is a hierarchical (thread-based) bulletin board application designed to facilitate smooth information exchange for life in New Zealand. By combining backend processing using PHP and MySQL with a modern UI via Tailwind CSS, I developed an environment where posts and replies feel near-instant and seamless.

02

Background & Purpose

This project was inspired by my plans to relocate to New Zealand in August 2026. I wanted to build a practical tool to help with local community integration and information gathering before and after my move, while also demonstrating my technical capabilities.

03

Key Focus: Risk Management & Security

IP Address Logging for Traceability

  • Purpose: To deter online harassment and spam caused by anonymous posting, ensuring a mechanism to identify the source in case of trouble.
  • Implementation: Utilizing PHP's $_SERVER['REMOTE_ADDR'], the system automatically logs the sender's IP address into the ip_address column of the database during every post or reply. This silently enhances security on the backend without compromising user convenience.

05

Design Philosophy & Commitment

Accessibility-First Design

Guided by the principle of "leaving no one behind," I prioritized high visual contrast and implemented an instant filtering search function using JavaScript. I focused intensely on making information easy to find and read for everyone.

Awareness of Legal Compliance

Beyond simple functionality, the design considers the legal and ethical aspects of operating a web service, such as requiring agreement to Terms of Service and logging IP addresses for safe community management.
